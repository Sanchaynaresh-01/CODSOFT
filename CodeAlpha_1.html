A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons program.

About the Legend

*Early Life: Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from a humble background and worked as a newspaper vendor to support his family.

*Education: He graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later earned a degree in aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

*Career in ISRO and DRDO: Kalam played a crucial role in India's space and missile programs. He was a key figure in the development of the SLV-3, India's first satellite launch vehicle, and the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

*Presidency: He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, earning the title "People's President" for his approachable nature.

*Visionary: Kalam was known for his vision of India as a developed nation by 2020, advocating for education, technology, and innovation.

*Author: He wrote several inspirational books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," focusing on youth empowerment and national development.

*Awards and Honors: Received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and various honorary doctorates.

*Legacy: Kalam's legacy continues to inspire millions, particularly youth, to pursue education and contribute to society. He emphasized the importance of dreams, hard work, and integrity.

*Death: He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a profound impact on the nation.

*Philosophy: Advocated for a vision of a peaceful and prosperous India, emphasizing the importance of education, ethics, and responsibility in leadership.
